HOW IT HAPPENED

The 1963 crash of a National Airways Corporation DC-3, with the loss of all 23 people on board, remains the worst air accident within New Zealand.
The flight departed from Auckland, bound for Wellington via Tauranga, Gisborne and Napier.
The weather was stormy, but forecasts underestimated the force of the wind.
During its descent into Tauranga, the plane was caught in a turbulent downdraught and slammed into a ridge of the Kaimai Range.

May the people that had died in this terrible airplane crash accident rest in peace.
